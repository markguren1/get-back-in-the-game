First Quarter: Master AI Fundamentals Without Becoming a Programmer

What You Actually Need to Know

You don't need to become a data scientist. You need to understand AI well enough to:

Essential AI Concepts (Non-Technical)

Context Windows: Understanding AI's "memory" limitations
Hallucinations: Recognizing when AI makes things up
Fine-Tuning vs. Prompting: Different ways to customize AI behavior

Hands-On Learning Path (Week-by-Week)

Week 1: Get Comfortable with ChatGPT/Claude
  • Sign up for ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro ($20/month)
  • Spend 30 minutes daily asking questions in your domain
  • Practice refining prompts to get better answers
Week 2: Learn Structured Prompting
  • Study prompt engineering basics (free resources on Anthropic's site)

  • Experiment with role-playing prompts: "You are an expert in [your field]..."
Week 3: Explore Specialized AI Tools
  • Research AI tools specific to your industry
  • Try 2-3 tools with free trials
  • Document what works and what doesn't
Week 4: Build Your First AI-Enhanced Project
  • Choose a real work challenge you've faced
  • Use AI to develop a solution or analysis
  • Document your process and results

Creating Portfolio Projects

Third Quarter: Network Strategically in the New Economy

The AI Networking Mindset

Traditional networking focused on "who you know." AI-era networking is about "what value you can create together."

Target Connections

Priority 1: AI-Forward Companies in Your Industry
  • Research companies actively investing in AI
  • Identify hiring managers and department heads
